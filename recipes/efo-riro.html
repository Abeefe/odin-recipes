Efo Riro Recipe

Efo Riro

Efo Riro is a rich vegetable soup that is native to the Yorubas of Western Nigeria. The vegetables that can be used to cook this soup are Efo Shoko or Efo Tete (Green Amaranth). If these are not readily available where you live, leafy or frozen spinach is a very good substitute.

Ingredients:

  • Assorted meat and fish. use a combination of the following:
    • Beef
    • Shaki (cow tripe)
    • Smoked fish
    • Dry fish
    • Stock fish
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 500g Efo Shoko
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons crayfish (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley or scallions (optional)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the leafy greens: Wash the leafy greens thoroughly and chop them into small pieces. Remove any stems or tough parts.
  2. Heat the oil: Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  3. Saute the onion, garlic, and ginger: Add the chopped onion, garlic, and ginger to the pot. Saute until the onion is translucent.
  4. Add the meat or fish: Add the meat or fish to the pot and cook until browned.
  5. Add the tomato puree and spices: Add the tomato puree, paprika, salt, and black pepper to the pot. Stir well.
  6. Add the water and crayfish (if using): Add the water and crayfish (if using) to the pot. Stir well.
  7. Add the leafy greens: Add the chopped leafy greens to the pot and stir well.
  8. Simmer the stew: Simmer the stew over low heat for 20-25 minutes or until the leafy greens are tender and the sauce has thickened.

I hope you enjoy this recipe for Efo Riro!
