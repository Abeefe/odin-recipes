Egusi Soup Recipe

Description

Egusi soup is native to West Africa (Nigeria), and many of my American and
Nigerian friends have enjoyed it. It is a great soup for those who like to
try something different every once in a while.

Ground Egusi seeds give this soup a unique color and flavor.
If you can't find Egusi seeds, you can substitute pumpkin seeds.
Any combination of crab, shrimp, and smoked fish can be used in place of the shrimp.
Drained, smoked oysters and chicken can be used in place of the beef.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup grounded egusi seeds(melon seeds)
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 pound beef or chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon grounded coriander
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups of chopped spinach or bitter leaves(optional)
  • 2 Scotch bonnet peppers, chopped(optional)

Steps:

  1. Rinse the grounded egusi seeds and soak them in water for about 30 minutes.
    Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat.
    Add the onion, garlic, and ginger. Saute until the onion is translucent.
  3. Add the beef or chicken and cook until browned, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the water, tomato paste, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  5. Add the soaked egusi seeds and stir well.
    Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low
    and simmer for20-25 minutes or until the soup thickens.
  6. Add the chopped spinach or bitter leaves and Scotch bonnet peppers (if using) to the pot.
    Stir well and simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
  7. Serve the egusi soup hot with fufu, rice, or any other preferred staple.

Tips:

  • You can roast the egusi seeds in a dry pan over low heat for 5-7 minutes
    to enhance their flavor before grinding them.
  • Use a blender or food processor to grind the egusi seeds into a fine powder.
  • Adjust the amount of water to achieve your desired consistency for the soup.
  • You can add other vegetables like bell peppers, carrots, or potatoes to the soup if you prefer.
  • Egusi soup is traditionally served with fufu, a staple made from cassava
    flour or plantain flour. You can also serve it with rice or other staples.

Enjoy your delicious egusi soup!
